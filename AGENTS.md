# Gurushala Website Developer Instructions

These instructions define the developer role for any coding agent or human developer working on this repository.

## Project identity

**Project name:** Gurushala Scheduled Caste Women Education Foundation

Gurushala is an India-based, Telnar/Kapadvanj, Scheduled Caste women-led education and cultural initiative. The website must present Gurushala as owned, led, and run by Scheduled Caste women.

External technical supporters may assist with setup, documentation, technical maintenance, and guidance only. Do not present any external supporter as founder, owner, trustee, director, or public face of Gurushala unless Gurushala formally approves this later.

## Language and tone

- Default website language: Gujarati.
- Use simple, village-friendly Gujarati where possible.
- Keep the tone dignified, practical, respectful, warm, and non-corporate.
- Avoid pity-based, dramatic, caste-exploitative, political, or savior-style language.
- English may be used only where needed for technical labels, URLs, or when specifically requested.

## Ownership and copyright

- Website content and public materials should be treated as belonging to Gurushala Scheduled Caste Women Education Foundation.
- Public wording should not imply ownership by Geyvak, Reva, Nitesh, or any external helper.
- Use ownership wording such as: "Copyright © Gurushala Scheduled Caste Women Education Foundation. All rights reserved." when footer or legal wording is needed.

## Website goals

The website should clearly communicate:

1. Gurushala's vision
2. Women-led governance
3. Education and cultural activities
4. Local community purpose
5. Document and registration readiness
6. Contact information
7. Updates and future development

## Design principles

- Mobile-first and readable on low-cost phones.
- Lightweight pages that load quickly on slower internet.
- Clean layout, large Gujarati text, high contrast, and simple navigation.
- Nature-inspired, calm, grounded visual direction.
- Avoid heavy animations, clutter, or complex UI.

## Safety and truthfulness rules

- Do not add fake registration numbers, fake directors, fake addresses, fake approvals, fake funding claims, or fake partnerships.
- If a legal/registration detail is unknown, use a clearly marked placeholder or ask for confirmation.
- Do not publish sensitive personal information.
- Do not add individual names unless the user or Gurushala explicitly provides and approves them.
- Ask before making major brand, structure, governance, or legal-content changes.

## Development rules

- Make small, reviewable changes.
- Keep code simple and maintainable.
- Preserve the existing working deployment unless a change is clearly safe.
- Test locally when possible before suggesting deployment.
- Summarize changed files after each task.
- Explain any risks before changing DNS, deployment settings, legal wording, or contact details.

## First Codex task

Before editing the website, inspect the current codebase and report:

1. What framework or structure the website uses
2. Which files control the homepage
3. What improvements are needed for Gujarati readability
4. What safe first changes are recommended
5. Whether the site appears ready for `gurushala.geyvak.com`

Do not make large edits until the plan is reviewed.
